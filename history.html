History of cats

Ancient Origins

Cats were first domesticated in the Near East around 10,000 years ago. The African wildcat (Felis silvestris lybica) is believed to be the ancestor of all domestic cats. Ancient civilizations, particularly the Egyptians, revered cats and kept them as sacred animals.

Egyptian Reverence

In ancient Egypt, cats were associated with the goddess Bastet and were protected by law. Harming a cat could result in severe punishment. Egyptians often mummified cats to honor them in the afterlife, and thousands of cat mummies have been discovered in archaeological sites.

Medieval and Modern Times

During the Middle Ages in Europe, cats were sometimes viewed with suspicion and association with witchcraft. However, they remained valuable for controlling rodent populations in homes and grain stores. By the Victorian era, cats became beloved household pets, and selective breeding developed many of the cat breeds we know today.

Cats Today

Today, cats are one of the most popular pets worldwide, valued for their companionship, independence, and affectionate nature. They continue to play an important role in human society, both as beloved family members and as working animals in pest control.
